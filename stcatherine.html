Saint Catherine

also known as Catherine of Alexandria, is a Christian martyr and saint who lived in the early 4th century. According to tradition, she was a princess and a noted scholar who converted to Christianity around the age of 141. She is said to have converted hundreds of people to Christianity and was martyred around the age of 18. Saint Catherine is venerated in the Eastern Orthodox Church, Catholic Church, Oriental Orthodox Churches, and Anglican Communion. Her feast day is celebrated on November 25th in the Catholic Church and November 24th in the Eastern Orthodox Church2.
